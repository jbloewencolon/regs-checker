     ARTICLE 8. CRIMES AGAINST CHASTITY, MORALITY AND DECENCY.

     §61-8-28a. Nonconsensual disclosure of private intimate images; definitions; and

            penalties.

1           (a) As used in this section:

2           (1) “Disclose” means to publish, publicly display, distribute, deliver, circulate or

3    disseminate by any means, including, but not limited to, electronic transmission.

4           (2) “Image” means a photograph, videotape, motion picture film, digital recording or any

5    product of any mechanical or electronic recording process or device that can preserve, for later

6    viewing, a visual image.

7           (3) “Intimate parts” means a person’s genitalia, pubic area, anus, or female post-

8    pubescent breasts.

9           (4) To “publicly disclose” means to disclose an image to one or more persons other than

10   those persons whom the person depicted understood would view the image at the time it was

11   captured.

12          (5) “Fabricated intimate image” means an image of an identifiable depicted individual that

13   was created by the use of artificial intelligence or other computer technology capable of

14   processing and interpreting specific data inputs and depicts computer-generated intimate parts

15   or the intimate parts of another human being as the intimate parts of the depicted individual.

16           (b) No person may knowingly and intentionally disclose, cause to be disclosed or threaten

17   to disclose, with the intent to harass, intimidate, threaten, humiliate, embarrass, or coerce, a

18   fabricated intimate image of another or an image of another which shows the intimate parts of the

19   depicted person or shows the depicted person engaged in sexually explicit conduct which was




                                                     2
     Enr CS for SB 198


20   captured under circumstances where the person depicted had a reasonable expectation that the

21   image would not be publicly disclosed.

22          (c)(1) A person convicted of a violation of subsection (b) of this section is guilty of a

23   misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be confined in jail for not more than one year,

24   fined not less than $1,000 nor more than $5,000, or both confined and fined.

25          (2) Notwithstanding the provisions of subdivision (1) of this subsection, a person convicted

26   of a second or subsequent violation of subsection (b) of this section is guilty of a felony and, upon

27   conviction thereof, shall be imprisoned in a state correctional facility for not more than three years,

28   fined not less than $2,500 nor more than $10,000, or both imprisoned and fined.

29          (d) The provisions of this section do not apply to:

30          (1) Images disclosed with the prior written consent of the person depicted;

31          (2) Images depicting the person voluntarily exposing himself or herself in a public or

32   commercial setting; or

33          (3) Disclosures made through the reporting of illegal conduct or the lawful and common

34   practices of law enforcement, criminal reporting, legal proceeding or medical treatment.

35          (e) Nothing in this section shall be construed to impose liability on the provider of an

36   interactive computer service as defined by 47 U. S. C. §230(f)(2), an information service as

37   defined by 47 U. S. C. §153(24), or telecommunications service as defined by 47 U. S. C.

38   §153(53), for content provided by another person.

     ARTICLE 8C. FILMING OF SEXUALLY EXPLICIT CONDUCT OF MINORS.

     §61-8C-1. Definitions.

1           (a) For the purposes of this article:

2           (1) “Minor” means any child under 18 years of age.

3           (2) “Knowledge” means knowing or having reasonable cause to know which warrants

4    further inspection or inquiry.

5           (3) “Sexually explicit conduct” includes any of the following, whether actually performed or

6    simulated:

7           (A) Genital to genital intercourse;

8           (B) Fellatio;

9           (C) Cunnilingus;

10          (D) Anal intercourse;

11          (E) Oral to anal intercourse;

12          (F) Bestiality;

13          (G) Masturbation;

14          (H) Sadomasochistic abuse, including, but not limited to, flagellation, torture, or bondage;

15          (I) Excretory functions in a sexual context; or

16          (J) Exhibition of the genitals, pubic, or rectal areas of any person in a sexual context.

17          (4) “Person” means an individual, partnership, firm, association, corporation, or other legal

18   entity: Provided, That this term does not apply to the provider of an interactive computer service

19   as defined by 47 U. S. C. §230(f)(2), an information service as defined by 47 U. S. C. §153(24),

20   or telecommunications service as defined by 47 U. S. C. §153(53), for content provided by another

21   person.

22          (5) “Visual portrayal” means:

23          (A) A photograph;

24          (B) A motion picture;

25          (C) A digital image;

26          (D) A digital video recording;

27          (E) Any other mechanical or electronic recording process or device that can preserve, for

28   later viewing, a visual image of a person that includes, but is not limited to, computers, cellphones,

29   personal digital assistance, and other digital storage or transmitting devices; or



                                                       4
     Enr CS for SB 198


30           (F) Any media listed in this subdivision which was created, generated, or otherwise

31   produced, in whole or in part, by any manner of manipulation, including, but not limited to, artificial

32   intelligence.

33           (6) “Computer-generated child pornography” means:

34           (A) Any visual portrayal of an identifiable minor that has been created, adapted, or

35   modified to depict the minor as engaging in sexually explicit conduct; or

36           (B) Any visual portrayal that appears to depict a minor engaged in sexually explicit conduct

37   if the visual portrayal is:

38           (i) Created by the use of artificial intelligence or other computer technology capable of

39   processing and interpreting specific data inputs to create a visual portrayal; and

40           (ii) Indistinguishable from a minor.

41           (7) “Identifiable minor” means any visual portrayal that depicts a minor who is identifiable

42   from the matter itself or from information displayed with or otherwise connected to the matter, and

43   that was created or altered by the use of artificial intelligence or other computer technology

44   capable of processing and interpreting specific data inputs to depict the minor.

45           (8) “Indistinguishable” as used with respect to a visual portrayal, means virtually

46   indistinguishable, in that the visual portrayal is such that an ordinary person viewing the visual

47   portrayal would conclude that the visual portrayal is of an actual minor engaged in sexually explicit

48   conduct. This definition does not apply to visual portrayals that are drawings, cartoons, sculptures,

49   or paintings depicting minors or adults.

50           (b) Nothing in this article shall be construed to include a visual portrayal of an actual and

51   identifiable person who was not a minor at the time the visual portrayal was created.

     §61-8C-2. Use of minors in filming sexually explicit conduct prohibited; penalty.

1            (a) Any person who causes or knowingly permits, uses, persuades, induces, entices, or

2    coerces such minor to engage in or uses such minor to do or assist in any sexually explicit conduct

3    is guilty of a felony when the act is being used to create a visual portrayal. Upon conviction thereof,

                                                       5
     Enr CS for SB 198


4    such person shall be fined not more than $10,000 or imprisoned in the penitentiary not more than

5    10 years, or both fined and imprisoned.

6           (b) Any person who creates a visual portrayal of a minor engaging in any sexually explicit

7    conduct is guilty of a felony and, upon conviction thereof, shall be fined not more than $10,000,

8    or imprisoned in the penitentiary not more than 10 years, or both fined and imprisoned.

9           (c) Any parent, legal guardian, or person having custody and control of a minor, who

10   creates a visual portrayal of such minor in any sexually explicit conduct or causes or knowingly

11   permits, uses, persuades, induces, entices, or coerces such minor child to engage in or assist in

12   any sexually explicit act is guilty of a felony when such act is used to create a visual portrayal.

13   Upon conviction thereof, such person shall be fined not more than $10,000 or imprisoned in the

14   penitentiary not more than 10 years, or both fined and imprisoned.

15          (d) It is not a defense under this section that the minor depicted has attained the age of at

16   least 18 years old at the time of investigation and/or prosecution, as long as the visual portrayal

17   of the minor used was originally taken or captured when the subject was less than 18 years of

18   age.

19          (e) It is not a defense under this section that the minor depicted is deceased at the time of

20   investigation and/or prosecution, regardless of whether the minor depicted had attained the age

21   of 18 years of age at the time of his or her death.

     §61-8C-3. Distribution and exhibiting of material depicting minors engaged in sexually

            explicit conduct or computer-generated child pornography prohibited; penalty.

1           (a) Any person who knowingly sends or causes to be sent or distributes, exhibits,

2    possesses, electronically accesses with intent to view or displays or transports any visual

3    portrayal of a minor engaged in any sexually explicit conduct or computer-generated child

4    pornography is guilty of a felony.

5           (b) It is sufficient for purposes of this section that the material visually portrays a minor,

6    regardless of whether the subject’s age is represented to be less than age 18 years old or whether

7    the minor subject’s actual identity can be ascertained.

8           (c) A visual portrayal created in whole or in part by digital manipulation, artificial

9    intelligence, or any other means may satisfy the requirements of this section.

10          (d) It is not a defense under this section that the minor depicted has attained the age of at

11   least 18 years old at the time of investigation and/or prosecution, as long as the visual portrayal

12   of the minor was originally taken or captured when the subject was under the age of 18 years of

13   age.

14          (e) It is not a defense under this section that the minor depicted is deceased at the time of

15   investigation and/or prosecution, regardless of whether the minor depicted had attained the age

16   of at least 18 years of age at the time of his or her death.

17          (f) Any person who violates the provisions of subsection (a) of this section when the

18   conduct involves 50 or fewer images shall, upon conviction, be imprisoned in a state correctional

19   facility for not less than two years nor more than five years or fined not more than $5,000 or both.

20          (g) Any person who violates the provisions of subsection (a) of this section when the

21   conduct involves more than 50 but fewer than 300 images shall, upon conviction, be imprisoned

22   in a state correctional facility for not less than three nor more than 15 years or fined not more than

23   $10,000, or both.

24          (h) Notwithstanding the provisions of subsections (b) and (c) of this section, any person

25   who violates the provisions of subsection (a) of this section when the conduct involves 300 or

26   more images or depicts violence against a child or a child engaging in bestiality shall, upon

27   conviction, be imprisoned in a state correctional facility for not less than five nor more than 20

28   years or fined not more than $25,000, or both.

     §61-8C-3a. Prohibiting child erotica; penalties.

1           (a) Any person age 18 or over who knowingly and intentionally produces, possesses,

2    displays or distributes, in any form, any visual portrayals of minors who are partially clothed, where

3    the visual portrayals are: (1) Unrelated to the sale of a commercially available legal product; and

4    (2) used for purely prurient purposes, is guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof,

5    shall be confined in jail for not more than one year or fined not more than $1,000, or both confined

6    and fined.

7           (b) As used in this section only:

8           (1) "Purely prurient purposes" means for the specific purpose of sexual gratification or

9    sexual arousal from viewing the visual portrayals prohibited by this section; and

10          (2) "Commercially available" means for sale to the general public.

     §61-8C-3b. Prohibiting juveniles from manufacturing, possessing, and distributing nude or

            partially nude images of minors; creating exemptions; declaring a violation to be

            an act of juvenile delinquency; and providing for the punishment thereof.

1           (a) Any minor who intentionally possesses, creates, produces, distributes, presents,

2    transmits, posts, exchanges, or otherwise disseminates any computer-generated child

3    pornography or a visual portrayal of another minor posing in an inappropriate sexual manner or

4    who distributes, presents, transmits, posts, exchanges, or otherwise disseminates a visual

5    portrayal of himself or herself posing in an inappropriate sexual manner is guilty of an act of

6    delinquency and, upon adjudication, disposition may be made by the circuit court pursuant to the

7    provisions of §49-4-701 through §49-4-725 of this code.

8            (b) As used in this section, “posing in an inappropriate sexual manner” means exhibition

9    of a bare female breast, female or male genitalia, pubic, or rectal areas of a minor for purposes

10   of sexual gratification.

11           (c) It shall be an affirmative defense to an alleged violation of this section that a minor

12   charged with possession of the prohibited visual portrayal did neither solicit its receipt nor

13   distribute, transmit, or present it to another person by any means.

14           (d) Notwithstanding the provisions of §15-12-1 et seq. of this code, an adjudication of

15   delinquency under the provisions of this section shall not subject the minor to the requirements of

16   that article and chapter.

     §61-8C-3c. Confidentiality; exemption from prosecution.

1            (a) Nothing in this article shall be construed to prevent the lawful investigation and/or

2    prosecution of the criminal offenses described in this article: Provided, That prohibited media or

3    visual portrayal described in this article shall not be published to the public at any time.

4            (b) It does not constitute an offense of the crimes set forth in this article when the following

5    persons possess or distribute prohibited media or material, or visual portrayal while acting in the

6    performance of their official duties:

7            (1) Law enforcement officials, including those entities with specialized investigatory

8    experience with whom law enforcement agencies regularly contract for the purpose of providing

9    investigatory services and assistance;

10           (2) Prosecuting attorneys;

11           (3) Attorneys acting as officers of the court and while acting in the performance of their

12     official duties;

13           (4) Judges and magistrates;

14           (5) Jurors hearing a case involving an alleged violation of offenses in this article;

15           (6) Support personnel for the persons listed in this section; and

     Enr CS for SB 198


16          (7) Any person acting in accordance and in compliance with a valid order issued by a

17   circuit court of this state or the Supreme Court of Appeals.

18          (c) The Supreme Court of Appeals is hereby requested to promulgate such rules,

19   protocols, and forms which are necessary to regulate access to, use, and handling of prohibited

20   media and visual portrayals described in this article, giving due consideration to the privacy rights

21   of victims and the due process rights of defendants in criminal proceedings.

22          (d)(1) Any person not listed in subsection (b) of this section who, in the course and scope

23   of employment or business, views an image or images on a computer or electronic device that is

24   or appears to be material visually portraying a minor engaged in any sexually explicit conduct

25   shall immediately report the discovery of the image or images to a local or state law-enforcement

26   agency or the Cyber Tipline at the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children. The report

27   must include the name and address of the owner or person claiming a right to possession of the

28   computer or electronic device, if known, and as permitted by federal law. For purposes of this

29   subdivision, such reporting may include furnishing the law enforcement officer with any image,

30   information, or data that the person reasonably believes to be evidence of material visually

31   portraying a minor engaged in any sexually explicit conduct, transmission of material visually

32   portraying a minor engaged in any sexually explicit conduct, or an image, information, or data that

33   is harmful to minors.

34          (2) Except in a case of willful or wanton misconduct, compliance with subdivision (1) of

35   this subsection is an affirmative defense to an alleged violation of this section.
