10                      SUBCHAPTER S.     ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

11            Sec.A2054.701.AADEFINITION.            In this subchapter, "unlawful

12   harm"    means     any   condition   in   which    the    use   of   an    artificial

13   intelligence system results in a consequential decision that causes

14   harm to an individual who is a member of a state or federally

15   protected class in violation of law.              The term does not include a

16   developer ’s or deployer ’s offer, license, or use of a heightened

17   scrutiny artificial intelligence system for the sole purpose of

18   testing the system before deployment to identify, mitigate, or

19   otherwise ensure compliance with state and federal law.

20            Sec.A2054.702.AAARTIFICIAL            INTELLIGENCE     SYSTEM          CODE    OF

21   ETHICS.    (a)     The department by rule shall establish an artificial

22   intelligence system code of ethics for use by state agencies and

23   local governments that procure, develop, deploy, or use artificial

24   intelligence systems.

25            (b)AAAt a minimum, the artificial intelligence system code of

26   ethics    must     include   guidance     for    the     deployment       and    use    of

27   artificial intelligence systems and heightened scrutiny artificial




                                               5
                                                                        S.B.ANo.A1964

1    intelligence systems that aligns with the Artificial Intelligence

2    Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) published by the National

3    Institute of Standards and Technology.             The guidance must address:

4                  (1)AAhuman oversight and control;

5                  (2)AAfairness and accuracy;

6                  (3)AAtransparency, including consumer disclosures;

7                  (4)AAdata privacy and security;

8                  (5)AApublic      and      internal           redress,      including

9    accountability and liability; and

10                 (6)AAthe frequency of evaluations and documentation of

11   improvements.

12           (c)AAState agencies and local governments shall adopt the

13   code of ethics developed under this section.

14           Sec.A2054.703.AAMINIMUM        STANDARDS     FOR    HEIGHTENED   SCRUTINY

15   ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEMS.            (a)   The department by rule shall

16   develop minimum risk management and governance standards for the

17   development,    procurement,     deployment,         and    use   of    heightened

18   scrutiny artificial intelligence systems by a state agency or local

19   government.

20           (b)AAThe    minimum   standards      must    be    consistent    with   the

21   Artificial    Intelligence    Risk   Management       Framework    (AI   RMF    1.0)

22   published by the National Institute of Standards and Technology and

23   must:

24                 (1)AAestablish     accountability            measures,     such    as

25   required   reports    describing     the     use    of,    limitations    of,   and

26   safeguards    for   the   heightened    scrutiny      artificial      intelligence

27   system;

                                                                           S.B.ANo.A1964

1                 (2)AArequire the assessment and documentation of the

2    heightened     scrutiny     artificial     intelligence             system ’s      known

3    security risks, performance metrics, and transparency measures:

4                       (A)AAbefore deploying the system; and

5                       (B)AAat the time any material change is made to:

6                               (i)AAthe system;

7                               (ii)AAthe state or local data used by the

8    system; or

9                               (iii)AAthe intended use of the system;

10                (3)AAprovide to local governments resources that advise

11   on   managing,   procuring,     and   deploying         a    heightened       scrutiny

12   artificial intelligence system, including data protection measures

13   and employee training; and

14                (4)AAestablish guidelines for:

15                      (A)AArisk management frameworks, acceptable use

19   frameworks     when   deploying       heightened            scrutiny        artificial

20   intelligence     systems   on     behalf    of    state          agencies    or    local

21   governments.

22          (c)AAState agencies and local governments shall adopt the

23   standards developed under Subsection (a).

23   the confidentiality of information submitted under this section,

24   including   restricting        access    to   submitted      information         to    only
